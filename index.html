"The Simpsons" (English "The Simpsons") is an American animated satirical series created by Matt Groening for the Fox company. The animated series is a satirical parody of the US middle-class lifestyle embodied in the Simpsons family, which consists of Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa and Maggie. Most of the events take place in the fictional town of Springfield. The show satirizes many aspects of people's lives, American culture, na society, even American television.

"The Simpsons" is the longest-running animated series in the history of American television: more than 700 episodes, which, in turn, are combined into 33 seasons of approximately 20 episodes each. He made his debut on April 19, 1987 on the Fox TV channel in The Tracey Ullman Show. The producer is the Gracie Films production company.

Groening invented The Simpsons shortly before he began working with James Brooks. He made several short stories about the life of a marginal family, choosing the names of his own family members for the characters, and replacing his own with "Bart". The shorts became part of The Tracey Ullman Show, which premiered on April 19, 1987. After three seasons on the air, the program evolved into a half-hour primetime show on December 17, 1989.
